Why does it matter?
Your score lets lenders know how likely you are to pay back borrowing, based on how you’ve handled it in the past. So, a higher score could mean you’re more likely to be offered borrowing at a lower interest rate.

It could help with things like getting a mortgage, renting a property, getting approved for credit cards or personal loans or just upgrading your mobile phone.

Ways of improving your credit
Take a look at some ways you could help build up your score.
Your credit history
Make your payments on time.
Aim to keep your credit utilisation below 25% of your credit limit.
Know your financial links, closing any unneeded joint accounts.
Your circumstances
Pay your bills on time.
Get on the electoral register to confirm your identity and address.
Set up direct debits for your regular payments.
