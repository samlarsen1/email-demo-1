What to expect from our emails

We will:
  • Always include your name and part of your postcode.
  • Sometimes include links to more information to help you.
  • Sometimes send you links to our secure app, to take you straight to the information you need.
We will never:
  • Send you to a web page that asks for your sign in, personal or banking details.
If you're not sure that an email is from us:
  • Forward it to security@halifax.co.uk and then delete it
  • Speak to us to check. You could do this by messaging us from your app or online banking.
We have lots of information to keep you and your money safe on our website. Just visit our Fraud Hub.

Don't fall for a scam
Remember, we'll never call to tell you to move money to another account or to ask for your full banking details. Unless you called for help, avoid any download that lets someone take control of your device. If you get a call like this, hang up. A genuine email from us will always include your title, surname and part of your account number or postcode. We'll never send an email with a link for you to log on or give personal and banking details. If you get a message like this, don't reply and delete it.

Calls and online sessions may be monitored and recorded. Not all Telephone Banking services are available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

If you need this communication in another format, such as large print, Braille or audio CD, please contact us.
You can call us using Relay UK if you have a hearing or speech impairment. There's more information on the Relay UK help pages. SignVideo services are also available if you're Deaf and use British Sign Language.
